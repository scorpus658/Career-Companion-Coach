
1. **Who exactly is this for?** 
	1. A 20-35 year old, job seeker, who is actively finding jobs in a deliberate and intentional way 

2. **The problem (in one para):**
	1. Job seekers spend 2-3 hours every day in manually finding relevant jobs (via WA and job boards) to their career goals and also applying across various job boards (e.g., Linkedin, Naukri).
	2. They spend another 1 hour tracking their applications in crude forms like excel, self WA group, etc. and also spend cognitive effort  in identifying the right referee to reach out to and in framing an appropriate message to them. 
	3. This process creates anxiety for the user around creating a strong funnel of job applications and also deprives them of the time better spent in interview preparation. 
	4. They also find this difficult to do when they are at the work place (inhibitions) and leaves bulk of the effort to be largely forced into evenings / hide time.
	
3. **The outcome (MVP bet) -** *The MVP is successful if it gives us conviction that we are onto a delta 4 change in the current status quo way for candidates* 
	1. We are able to see that users have **meaningfully** saved time in finding relevant applications and keeping track of them 
	2. The users express a step change (need to quantify) in the way their preferences are understood
	3. The users express distinctive delight (need to quantify this) in seeing jobs that are most relevant to their stated and (unstated) career preferences
